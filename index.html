How to Align Your Course

1. Measurable Objectives

An objective states what students should be able to do. Avoid "internal" verbs like understand, know, learn, or appreciate. You cannot measure what is inside a student's head.

Use Bloom's Verbs: Identify, Describe, Analyze, Evaluate, Create.

2. Course Activities

Every activity, reading, and assessment must exist to help the student achieve the stated objectives. If an activity is purely "busy work", it breaks alignment.

3. The Alignment Check

Perfect alignment means:
✓ Every objective is measured by at least one activity.
✓ Every activity directly supports an objective.
